Conference/Journal 2023

OP-Follower: Onboard Vision-based Obstacle-Aware Predictive Person Following for Quadruped Robots via IBVS-NMPC Control

Junwei Ge, Jinya Su*, Shihua Li
School of Automation, Key Laboratory of Measurement and Control of CSE, Ministry of Education, and Institute of Intelligent Unmanned Systems, South-east University, Nanjing 210096, China.
Paper Code

OP-Follower is a fully onboard, vision-based framework that enables quadruped robots to follow a person and avoid obstacles using only visual inputs, integrating open-vocabulary perception, predictive tracking, and safety-constrained control.

Abstract

This paper presents OP-Follower, a fully onboard, vision-based, obstacle-aware person-following framework for quadruped robots. Unlike existing approaches that rely on LiDAR, external communication sensors, or offboard computation, OP-Follower operates exclusively on visual inputs and runs entirely on a Unitree Go2 equipped with a Jetson Orin NX. The proposed system bridges the gap between delayed visual perception and safety-aware control by integrating YOLO-World-based open-vocabulary person detection, BoxMOT tracking, and Kalman filter-based predictive state estimation for delay compensation, within a unified planning-control architecture that combines image-based visual servoing (IBVS), nonlinear model predictive control (NMPC), and artificial potential field (APF)-based obstacle avoidance. Comprehensive evaluations in both high-fidelity Gazebo simulations and real-world indoor and outdoor environments, including long-distance following, dynamic pursuit--evasion tasks, and multi-person scenarios, demonstrate robust real-time performance, achieving an average tracking error of xx~m with fully onboard processing at 50Hz closed-up frequency. To the best of our knowledge, OP-Follower represents the first pure vision-based quadruped resilient person-following system that jointly integrates open-vocabulary perception, predictive visual tracking, and safety-constrained NMPC with decoupled obstacle avoidance, providing a practical and scalable solution for real-world human-robot interaction on resource-constrained platforms.
